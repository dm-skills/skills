## Format Copy Patterns

Use this reference when the user needs practical copy structures by format rather than abstract copywriting advice.

---

## 4x6 Postcard

Best for:
- tight offers
- simple local promotions
- reminders
- low-complexity retention messages

Recommended structure:
1. Headline with one clear benefit
2. Offer line
3. One short proof point
4. CTA

Rule:
- if the message needs a paragraph to work, this is probably the wrong format

---

## 6x9 Postcard

Best for:
- acquisition
- win-back
- stronger visual storytelling
- moderate product or service explanation

Recommended structure:
1. Strong headline
2. Offer or core value area
3. Two to three supporting bullets
4. Social proof or trust cue
5. CTA with one primary response path

This size often gives enough room to explain the offer without losing scanability.

---

## 6x11 Postcard

Best for:
- competitive prospecting
- higher-value offers
- multi-benefit positioning
- bold visual merchandising

Recommended structure:
1. Big front-side value proposition
2. Secondary proof or comparison
3. Back-side detail with bullets or short sections
4. Large CTA and response mechanisms

Use the extra space to sharpen clarity, not to dump extra copy.

---

## Letter Package

Best for:
- high-consideration decisions
- relationship building
- storytelling
- financial, healthcare, nonprofit, or B2B messages

Recommended structure:
1. Opening that signals relevance immediately
2. Problem or opportunity
3. Why the sender is reaching out now
4. Offer or invitation
5. Proof
6. Clear next step

Letter copy can be more conversational, but every paragraph still needs a job.

---

## Self-Mailer

Best for:
- educational selling
- product collections
- local seasonal programs
- mixed editorial and promotional messages

Recommended structure:
1. Cover promise
2. Interior explanation or merchandising
3. Offer or key decision prompt
4. CTA repeated in more than one panel

Self-mailers work best when each panel has a clear role rather than one continuous wall of text.

---

## Folded Mailers

Best for:
- staged storytelling
- reveal mechanics
- value builds across panels

Recommended structure:
- Panel 1: hook
- Panel 2: problem or value framing
- Panel 3: proof or benefit cluster
- Final panel: CTA and response details

Treat folds as sequencing tools. The order of discovery matters.

---

## Local-Service Mail

Common structure:
1. Local relevance cue
2. Problem the recipient likely recognizes
3. One practical solution or offer
4. Trust marker
5. Appointment CTA

Trust markers that usually help:
- nearby service footprint
- review count or rating
- guarantee or service promise
- category credential

---

## B2B Mail

Common structure:
1. Business problem or cost
2. Why this company is likely to care
3. One credible angle of differentiation
4. Invitation to talk, assess, or review
5. Clear response path

B2B copy usually improves when it sounds more specific and more operational, not more clever.

---

## Copy QA by Format

Before finalizing any format, confirm:
- the first read communicates the benefit fast enough for the format size
- the CTA matches the level of commitment the format can support
- proof and detail are proportional to the decision complexity
- legal copy is present but not allowed to bury the selling message
