# Creative Reference

Use this file when the main creative workflow needs production-level detail.

## Format-Specific Design Rules

### 4x6 postcard

- Front should communicate one idea, one image, and one message.
- Back usually carries the offer box, QR code, short URL, promo code, and address panel.
- Keep critical content at least 0.25 inches inside trim.
- Extend background 0.125 inches beyond trim for bleed.

### 6x9 postcard

- Good balance of impact and cost.
- Support a stronger headline, short body copy, and benefit bullets.
- Back can hold the offer box, QR code, URL, promo code, and address panel with less crowding.

### 6x11 postcard

- Premium format with room for long-form copy or multiple product blocks.
- Best for higher-value offers, richer storytelling, or high-LTV audiences.

### Letter package

- Outer envelope should create curiosity without feeling deceptive.
- Letter should use short paragraphs, strong white space, and a clear signature block.
- Include a P.S. line.
- Inserts should work as standalone summaries of the offer.

### Self-mailer

- Outer panel must sell the open.
- Inner spread can support product clusters, education, or multi-step storytelling.
- Response panel should make the next action obvious.

## Paper and Print Specs

### Stock guidance

- Standard postcards: 14pt coated stock, gloss or matte
- Premium postcards: 16pt coated stock, often matte or soft-touch
- Letters: 60 to 70lb uncoated text
- Outer envelopes: 24lb bond
- Inserts: 80lb coated text
- Self-mailers: 80 to 100lb coated text

### Color and image rules

- Design in CMYK, not RGB.
- Use spot or Pantone workflows only when the printer supports them and the brand color is critical.
- Keep small black text on 100 percent K for sharpness.
- Use at least 300 DPI at final size.
- Prefer vector logos.

## Typography and Readability

- Body copy should usually be at least 10pt, and larger for older audiences.
- Legal copy should remain legible even when compact.
- Headlines should read clearly at arm's length.
- CTA text should be one of the most visually prominent elements.
- Serif often suits traditional or trust-heavy categories.
- Sans-serif often suits modern consumer brands.
- Avoid decorative scripts, ultra-light weights, and low-contrast copy on busy imagery.

## Compliance Reference

### Universal requirements

- Physical return address
- Correct indicia or postage marking
- Clear address area
- Opt-out suppression and address hygiene

### Financial services

- Product disclosures
- Banking or lending identifiers where required
- Terms and restrictions
- Dedicated compliance review before print

### Healthcare and pharmaceutical

- Claim substantiation
- Fair-balance requirements where applicable
- Careful handling of any sensitive personalization

### Sweepstakes or contest offers

- No-purchase-necessary language when required
- Entry method, prize value, and timing details
- Geographic eligibility and rules access

### Claim-language guardrails

Avoid unsupported superlatives, unverifiable comparisons, or health and environmental claims without support. Prefer precise and supportable language tied to evidence, awards, testimonials, or studies.

## Creative QA Checklist

### Copy QA

- Headline communicates the main value fast
- Offer includes amount, qualifier, and deadline
- CTA includes the exact response mechanism
- P.S. restates the offer for letters
- Personalization fallbacks are confirmed
- Legal copy is reviewed when needed

### Design QA

- Files exported in CMYK
- Images meet print resolution
- Bleed and safe zones are correct
- Address area stays clear
- QR code scans correctly
- Fonts are embedded or outlined
- Proof is reviewed and approved

### Tracking QA

- Promo codes are mapped by cell
- URLs resolve and track correctly
- QR and typed URL destinations match
- Phone or in-store redemption paths are tested

## Structural Templates

### Postcard back

- Headline reinforcement
- Short body copy
- Offer box
- QR code and short URL
- Clear address block area
- Fine print placed without crowding the main offer

### Letter opening patterns

- Empathy open tied to a specific frustration
- Story open anchored in one concrete example
- Direct-offer open for warmer audiences
- Win-back acknowledgment for lapsed customers
