## Channel Economics

Use this reference when the user needs a practical way to compare direct mail operating modes, audience sources, and rollout economics without collapsing everything into a single cost-per-piece number.

---

## Compare the Operating Mode Before You Compare the Format

Direct mail economics depend on how the program is bought, targeted, and repeated. Start with the operating mode:

| Mode | Best Use | Economic Strength | Main Tradeoff |
|------|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Standalone campaign | Prospecting, seasonal pushes, win-back | Flexible strategy and creative control | Higher planning overhead and slower iteration |
| Triggered or programmatic mail | Lifecycle, cart abandonment, replenishment, onboarding | Strong timing relevance and easier automation | Lower volume per drop and more systems work |
| Local saturation or neighborhood mail | Local services, franchise, retail footprint | Broad reach and easy geography-based rollout | Weaker precision than customer-file targeting |
| Insert or bundled media | Offer amplification, sampling, broad awareness | Lower unit economics at larger scale | Less control over environment and recipient intent |
| Long-form or catalog-style mail | Multi-product merchandising, inspiration, retention | Higher average order value and discovery | Larger upfront creative and print cost |

Do not frame these as branded product categories. Frame them as operating models with different cost structures and response expectations.

## Audience Source Drives Economics

Format matters, but audience source often matters more.

| Audience Source | Typical Role | Why It Can Win | Main Risk |
|-----------------|--------------|----------------|-----------|
| House file | Retention, win-back, cross-sell | Highest relevance and easiest measurement | Limited scale |
| Modeled prospect list | Acquisition | Better efficiency than broad cold outreach | Model quality varies widely |
| Reverse append or identity resolution | Anonymous-site or CRM-light environments | Unlocks reachable postal audiences | Match quality and privacy/governance need review |
| Event-based or intent-based audiences | Moving, new homeowner, life event, behavior | Strong timing signal | Signal freshness degrades fast |
| Geographic selection | Local service and retail | Easy to explain and scale | Can hide weak unit economics in broad areas |

When choosing among sources, ask:
- What signal makes this audience likely to respond now?
- How fast does that signal decay?
- Can we scale the source if the pilot works?
- Can we explain why the economics should improve or hold at rollout volume?

## Economics Questions to Answer in Every Plan

Before recommending any program, answer:

1. What does it cost to learn?
2. What does it cost to scale?
3. Which part of the cost stack is fixed versus volume-sensitive?
4. Which response assumptions are directional versus decision-grade?
5. What outcome would justify the next phase?

A useful planning view includes:

| Lever | Pilot View | Rollout View |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| Quantity | Small enough to learn | Large enough to improve unit economics |
| Cost per piece | Often elevated | Usually lower at scale |
| Response expectation | Wide confidence band | Narrower once learnings are applied |
| Audience precision | Often tighter | May broaden as scale increases |
| Creative complexity | May stay controlled | Can expand only after a winning frame is clear |

## Proposal-Grade Optioning

When presenting options, show them side by side instead of hiding tradeoffs.

Useful option structures:
- Conservative option: smaller audience, tighter targeting, more defensible economics
- Balanced option: reasonable learning breadth with manageable spend
- Expansion option: higher volume or wider coverage to estimate scalability

For each option, include:
- audience source
- quantity
- format
- estimated all-in cost per piece
- estimated total cost
- the major hypothesis it is testing
- what rollout economics may look like if the test is successful

This makes the plan useful for real decision-making instead of only tactical execution.

## Planning Table Shape

This table pattern works well for both internal planning and stakeholder review:

| Cell or Option | Hypothesis | Audience Source | Format | Key Variable | Quantity | Est. CPP | Rollout CPP | Total Cost | Decision Use |
|----------------|------------|-----------------|--------|--------------|----------|----------|-------------|------------|--------------|
| Control | Best current thesis | [source] | [format] | Baseline | [N] | $X.XX | $X.XX | $XX,XXX | Establish baseline |
| Challenger A | Tests strongest alternative | [source] | [format] | Offer or message | [N] | $X.XX | $X.XX | $XX,XXX | Choose offer or message |
| Challenger B | Tests scalability or format | [source] | [format] | Audience or format | [N] | $X.XX | $X.XX | $XX,XXX | Choose expansion path |

The important point is not the exact labels. The important point is making the economics and the learning agenda visible in the same table.
