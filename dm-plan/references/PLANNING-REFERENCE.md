# Planning Reference

Use this file when you need planning detail that would otherwise bloat the main skill.

## Cost Stack Benchmarks

### List and data costs

- Owned CRM list: usually no acquisition cost
- Prospect list rental: often $50 to $400 CPM depending on source and selectivity
- Data hygiene such as NCOA and CASS: often $5 to $20 CPM
- List enhancement or append work: often $0.05 to $0.25 per record

### Creative costs

- Simple postcard design: often $500 to $2,000
- Letter-package design: often $1,500 to $4,000
- Copywriting: often $500 to $3,000
- Photography or illustration: highly variable
- Triggered-mail HTML template work: often $500 to $1,500

### Print production

- 4x6 postcard: often $0.05 to $0.12 per piece
- 6x9 postcard: often $0.08 to $0.18 per piece
- 6x11 postcard: often $0.12 to $0.22 per piece
- Letter package: often $0.25 to $0.50 per piece
- Self-mailer: often $0.15 to $0.35 per piece
- Dimensional mailer: often $1.50 to $8.00 per piece

### Postage

- First Class: premium timing and address handling
- Marketing Mail: lower cost, slower timing
- Local saturation programs: efficient for broad geographic reach

### Mail house services

- Addressing or inkjetting: often $10 to $25 per thousand
- Tabbing, folding, or inserting: often $20 to $50 per thousand
- Postage handling: often $5 to $15 per thousand
- Minimum charges: often $200 to $500 per job

## ROI Model Template

```text
Campaign ROI Model

Inputs
- Mail quantity
- All-in cost per piece
- Total campaign cost
- Expected response rate
- Expected conversion rate
- Expected AOV
- Gross margin percentage

Outputs
- Expected responders
- Expected conversions
- Expected revenue
- Expected gross profit
- Raw ROAS
- Raw CPA

Incrementality adjustment
- Expected holdout conversion percentage
- Incremental conversion rate
- Incremental conversions
- Incremental revenue
- Incremental ROAS
- Incremental CPA
```

## Break-Even Template

Use:

```text
Break-even response rate = total campaign cost / (AOV x gross margin)
```

Check the result against:
- realistic benchmark range for the audience and format
- pessimistic and base-case scenarios
- the size of the planned holdout and test cells

## Budget Allocation Rules of Thumb

- Small programs often devote most of the spend to print and postage, with lean creative and limited analysis.
- Mid-sized programs usually allow stronger creative, list work, and measurement.
- Larger programs should reserve explicit budget for testing, measurement, and scaled rollout planning.

Use allocation guidance only as a starting point. Audience quality and format choice matter more than generic percentage splits.

## Lead-Time Reference

### List work

- Owned CRM pull: often 1 to 3 days
- Prospect list rental: often 3 to 7 business days
- NCOA processing: often 2 to 5 business days
- Complex modeled list: often 1 to 3 weeks

### Print production

- Digital short-run print below 5,000 pieces: often 3 to 5 business days
- Offset print in the 5,000 to 50,000 range: often 7 to 10 business days
- Special finishes or complex folds: often 10 to 15 business days
- Dimensional packages: often 15 to 25 business days

### Mail house

- Basic addressing and postage handling: often 3 to 5 business days
- Multi-component inserting: often 5 to 10 business days
- Variable-data production: often 5 to 7 business days

### Delivery

- First Class: often 1 to 5 business days
- Marketing Mail: often 3 to 10 business days
- Local saturation programs: often 3 to 10 business days depending on entry point and postal facility
