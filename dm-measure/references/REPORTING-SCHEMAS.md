## Reporting Schemas

Use this reference when the user needs a practical reporting pack structure for pilots, scaled campaigns, or always-on mail programs.

---

## Reporting Pack Layers

A strong reporting pack usually includes four layers:

1. Delivery and execution health
2. Attributed performance
3. Incremental performance
4. Decision-ready interpretation

Do not jump straight to ROAS without showing whether the file actually mailed cleanly and whether the response read is mature enough to trust.

---

## Interim Readout Schema

Use this when the campaign is still in flight and the response curve is incomplete.

Recommended sections:
- mailed quantity by cell
- delivered or processed quantity if available
- early attributed orders or leads
- promo-code usage
- matchback volume
- response rate by cell
- percent through expected response curve
- projected final orders or leads
- projected CPA or ROAS
- early recommendation

Useful table:

| Cell | Mailed | Responses to Date | Response Rate | Percent Through Curve | Projected Final Responses | Projected CPA | Read Quality |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------------|-----------------------|---------------------------|---------------|--------------|
| Control | [N] | [N] | X.XX% | XX% | [N] | $X | Directional or stable |
| Challenger | [N] | [N] | X.XX% | XX% | [N] | $X | Directional or stable |

Interim reporting should make it obvious whether the result is mature enough for a decision.

## Final Campaign Report Schema

Use this when the response window is substantially complete.

Recommended sections:
- campaign objective
- audience and cell design
- mailed volume and timing
- attributed results
- holdout or control comparison
- incremental lift
- margin-aware economics
- recommendation for scale, pause, or retest

Useful summary table:

| Cell | Mailed | Attributed Responses | Incremental Lift | Incremental CPA | Incremental ROAS | Recommendation |
|------|--------|----------------------|------------------|-----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Control | [N] | [N] | baseline | $X | X.XX | Maintain or replace |
| Challenger | [N] | [N] | +X% | $X | X.XX | Scale or retest |

## Always-On Triggered Reporting

Triggered programs need recurring reporting instead of one final campaign summary.

Recommended cadence:
- weekly operational review
- 30-day performance summary
- 60-day maturation check
- 90-day stabilized readout for higher-latency outcomes

Recommended sections:
- trigger volume
- successful sends
- suppressed records
- addressable rate
- conversion by trigger type
- holdout comparison if available
- trend versus prior period
- issue log and corrective actions

---

## Operational Diagnostics Schema

Use this when performance is weak and the team needs to separate strategy issues from execution issues.

Diagnostic sections:
- file count in versus mailed count out
- suppression rate
- address quality or undeliverable indicators
- variant or template mix
- drop timing
- response tracking gaps
- conversion lag versus expected curve

Helpful question set:
- Did fewer records mail than planned?
- Did one cell or region under-mail?
- Did the offer code or response path fail?
- Is the underperformance broad or isolated?

---

## Through-Curve Forecasting Notes

Early reporting is useful when it is honest about maturity.

Good practice:
- state the share of the expected response curve completed
- project only when historical response timing is reasonably stable
- separate observed from projected metrics
- update the projection window consistently across all cells

Poor practice:
- treating a 10-day read like a final result
- projecting off tiny counts without showing uncertainty
- comparing cells with different in-home timing as if they were equally mature
