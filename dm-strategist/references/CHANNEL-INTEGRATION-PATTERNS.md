## Channel Integration Patterns

Use this reference when the user needs to integrate direct mail with adjacent channels instead of planning it in isolation.

---

## Direct Mail Plus Email

Best for:
- retention
- win-back
- timed promotions
- post-mail reinforcement

Common sequence:
1. Mail creates attention and credibility.
2. Email follows with convenience and reminder value.
3. Landing page or checkout captures the response cleanly.

Good uses:
- send email shortly after expected in-home timing
- reinforce the same core offer rather than introducing a different one
- use email to catch recipients who saw the mail but delayed action

Main risk:
- conflicting offers across channels that muddy the readout

---

## Direct Mail Plus Paid Social

Best for:
- acquisition
- household awareness
- creative reinforcement
- regional or local campaigns

Common sequence:
1. Mail introduces the offer or brand promise.
2. Paid social increases frequency and recall.
3. The landing page or store visit closes the loop.

Good uses:
- use the same headline or visual concept across channels
- coordinate flight timing around expected mail delivery
- use paid social to reinforce high-value geographic clusters

Main risk:
- taking credit away from mail or social because attribution rules are not aligned

---

## Direct Mail Plus Search or Landing Page Capture

Best for:
- high-intent categories
- research-heavy purchases
- local services
- financial or healthcare decisions

Common sequence:
1. Mail creates the initial motivation.
2. Recipient searches the brand or offer.
3. Branded search or a focused landing page captures the conversion.

Good uses:
- align search copy with the mail promise
- make the landing page reflect the same offer and proof points
- track vanity URLs, QR codes, and matchback where possible

Main risk:
- measuring only direct URL or code responses and missing the actual halo

---

## Direct Mail Plus Broadcast or Audio

Best for:
- regional campaigns
- brand-led acquisition
- local retail and service brands

Common sequence:
1. Broadcast or audio builds familiarity.
2. Mail turns broad awareness into a specific action.

This combination often works when the broadcast channel warms the market and the mail piece gives the recipient a concrete reason to act.

---

## Triggered Mail Plus Lifecycle Channels

Best for:
- onboarding
- replenishment
- cart abandonment
- post-event follow-up

Common sequence:
1. Trigger fires from behavior or time-based condition.
2. Mail arrives as a high-salience offline touch.
3. Email, SMS, or app messaging continues the sequence if consent exists.

The key design rule is sequence coherence. Each channel should feel like part of one conversation, not disconnected campaigns.

---

## Channel Choice Questions

When deciding whether to integrate channels, ask:
- Is direct mail doing the awareness job, the trust job, or the conversion job?
- Which adjacent channel adds convenience without replacing the role of mail?
- Can the measurement plan explain how the channels interact?
- Will the added channel improve economics, or only add noise?
