# Vertical Playbooks

Use this file when a strategy question depends heavily on industry dynamics.

## E-Commerce

**Profile:** Online retailer with a digital-first customer base using mail as an additional acquisition, retention, or win-back channel.

**Key dynamics:**
- Clean purchase data usually makes holdout and matchback easier
- Physical mail can cut through digital noise
- Seasonal cycles matter

**Recommended entry point:**
- Start with retention or win-back before broad acquisition
- Use the house file first

**Typical format choices:**
- Retention: 6x9 postcard with a tailored offer
- Win-back: letter or postcard depending on brand tone
- Acquisition: postcard first, letter when customer value is higher

**Measurement note:**
- Baseline purchase rates can be high, so holdout-based lift matters more than raw attribution

## Financial Services

**Profile:** Credit, insurance, mortgage, lending, wealth, or adjacent trust-heavy categories.

**Key dynamics:**
- Disclosure-heavy workflow
- Longer consideration window
- Higher LTV often justifies higher mailing cost

**Recommended entry point:**
- Retention and cross-sell before broad acquisition

**Typical format choices:**
- Letter packages for acquisition and higher-trust products
- Postcards for some existing-customer communication

**Measurement note:**
- Expect longer windows and stronger need for control design

## Non-Profit

**Profile:** Fundraising, advocacy, membership, or donor-development programs.

**Key dynamics:**
- Storytelling is often stronger than feature-heavy offers
- Donor LTV matters more than first-gift economics
- Seasonal fundraising cycles are important

**Recommended entry point:**
- Evaluate donor acquisition on long-term value, not immediate payback alone

**Typical format choices:**
- Letter packages for acquisition and renewal
- Higher-touch packages for major-donor cultivation

**Measurement note:**
- Track donor acquisition cost and donor value over time

## B2B

**Profile:** SaaS, services, manufacturing, or account-based outreach to business decision-makers.

**Key dynamics:**
- Lower volume and higher cost per piece
- Named-recipient personalization matters
- Mail often performs best as one step in a broader ABM sequence

**Recommended entry point:**
- Focus on high-value accounts and clear meeting or pipeline goals

**Typical format choices:**
- Letter or premium package for prospecting
- Personalized follow-up mail for ABM programs

**Measurement note:**
- Measure meetings, opportunities, or qualified pipeline, not just response volume
