## Diagnostic Decision Trees

Use this reference when the user needs structured troubleshooting guidance after weak performance, mixed results, or uncertainty about whether to scale, pause, or retest.

---

## Low Response Diagnostic

If results are weak, work through this order:

1. Did the intended audience actually mail at the expected volume?
2. Was in-home timing aligned with the offer window?
3. Was the response path clean and easy to use?
4. Did the audience have a credible reason to care now?
5. Was the offer strong enough for the cost and friction of response?
6. Was the format capable of carrying the message?

This sequence prevents teams from blaming creative when the real issue is file quality, timing, or audience choice.

---

## Scale Decision Tree

When a test looks positive:

1. Is the result mature enough to trust?
2. Is the economic read positive on an incremental basis?
3. Are rollout assumptions grounded in realistic scaled costs?
4. Can the audience or geography expand without losing the original signal?
5. Can operations and reporting support the larger program?

If any answer is weak, recommend a narrower expansion or replication test rather than a full rollout.

---

## Triggered Program Diagnostic

When triggered mail underperforms:

1. Is the trigger arriving close enough to the qualifying event?
2. Is the triggered audience large enough to generate stable learning?
3. Are suppression and addressability reducing volume more than expected?
4. Does the mail piece reflect the trigger context clearly?
5. Is the downstream conversion path consistent with the recipient's stage?

Triggered programs often fail because of timing and systems design, not only creative quality.

---

## Creative vs Audience Diagnosis

To separate creative weakness from audience weakness:

- If multiple cells underperform across one audience source, question the audience first.
- If one creative frame loses while the audience and offer stay steady, question the message.
- If a stronger offer wins inside the same audience, the audience may be viable but insufficiently motivated.
- If a different audience wins with similar creative, scale the audience insight before rewriting the campaign.

---

## Operations vs Strategy Diagnosis

Use this split when results are confusing:

Operations questions:
- Was the file complete and clean?
- Did the correct version mail to the correct cell?
- Were codes, URLs, and QR destinations working?
- Did mail drop on the intended schedule?

Strategy questions:
- Was the audience the right one?
- Was the offer compelling enough?
- Was the format appropriate for the message?
- Was the measurement method strong enough to read the result?

The point of the diagnostic is not just to explain what happened. It is to identify the most useful next move.
